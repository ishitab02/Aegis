## Our Solution (What AEGIS Does)

Think of AEGIS like a **smart home security system**, but for crypto applications.

### The Three Watchers (SentinelSwarm)

We have three AI "security guards" that watch different things:

| Guard | What They Watch | Real-World Analogy |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Liquidity Sentinel** | Is money leaving too fast? | Watching the vault - if someone's emptying it quickly, something's wrong |
| **Oracle Sentinel** | Are the prices accurate? | Checking if the cash register is showing the right amounts |
| **Governance Sentinel** | Is someone changing the rules illegally? | Watching for someone trying to change the locks without permission |

### How They Work Together

1. Each guard watches independently (so one can't be tricked)
2. If **2 out of 3** guards agree something is wrong → alarm triggers
3. The system automatically **freezes** the application (like a bank vault locking down)
4. Everything happens in **seconds**, not hours

### The Detective (ChainSherlock)

After the alarm triggers, our AI detective:
- **Traces** exactly what the hacker did
- **Identifies** how they broke in
- **Tracks** where the money went
- **Generates** a report in minutes (not days)

## FAQs

### "Isn't this just another crypto project?"

No. We're not making a cryptocurrency or asking you to buy tokens. We're building security infrastructure - like selling locks and alarm systems during a gold rush.

### "What if hackers attack AEGIS itself?"

Good question. That's why we use:
1. **Decentralized infrastructure** (no single server to attack)
2. **Multiple independent AI agents** (can't trick all three)
3. **Chainlink's security** (same tech protecting billions of dollars already)

### "Why hasn't someone built this already?"

The technology just became available:
- Chainlink CRE (our main platform) launched November 2025
- x402 payments (our revenue model) launched late 2025
- AI models capable of this analysis only matured recently

We're building at exactly the right time.

### "How is this different from antivirus software?"

Antivirus looks for known threats. AEGIS looks for **anomalies** - unusual patterns that might indicate a new type of attack. It's more like fraud detection at a bank than antivirus on your computer.

### "Can't hackers just work around it?"

They can try. But:
1. We're watching multiple signals simultaneously (hard to fake all of them)
2. We require 2/3 agreement (can't trick just one AI)
3. We freeze first, ask questions later (stops attack even if new type)
4. We learn from every attack (system gets smarter)

It's an arms race, but right now the attackers are winning because there's NO defense. We change that.
